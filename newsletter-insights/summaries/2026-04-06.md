### A Guide to Context Engineering for LLMs `AI/ML`
- More context can actually make LLMs dumber — a 2025 Chroma study found all 18 tested models (GPT-4.1, Claude, Gemini) performed worse as input length increased
- Accuracy degradation was severe: some models held 95% accuracy then nosedived to 60% once input exceeded a threshold
- Context engineering is the discipline of structuring information optimally; more context is not always better
- LLMs process information through tokens (chunks averaging ~3/4 word), context windows (total tokens visible in one interaction), and attention mechanisms (how model connects related tokens)
- Modern context windows range 128K to 2M+ tokens, but architectural blind spots mean what you include and how you structure it matters far more than volume

### You're Not a Bad Communicator. You're a Diplomat in a Room Full of Commanders. `Product`
- ExecReps built acoustic voice analysis measuring pace, filler words, clarity, confidence—but users reported high scores without feeling confident as communicators
- The score trap: numeric feedback satisfies competence but misses relatedness and autonomy; people optimize for identity ("Who am I as a communicator?") not just improvement metrics
- Self-Determination Theory shows scores miss two of three psychological needs; fitness apps fail because they measure calories burned instead of fostering "athlete" identity
- Existing personality frameworks (DISC, Myers-Briggs, Big Five) don't translate well to voice analysis; they were built for surveys, not acoustic properties
- Four communication dimensions emerged from analyzing actual pressure recordings: Command (among others), suggesting a new framework tailored to speech rather than borrowed from psychology questionnaires
