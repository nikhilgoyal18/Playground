### How to prepare for AI PM interviews `Product`
- LinkedIn retired its APM program and replaced it with a **Full Stack Builder track** — applicants must have shipped real products using AI tools, not just list AI familiarity on a resume.
- Google India and Figma now run **45-minute live build rounds** where candidates prototype in Bolt, Emergent, or v0 while narrating their thinking aloud.
- Meta added a **"Product Sense with AI" round** that replaced its standalone technical interview; OpenAI and Anthropic ask product sense questions specifically about AI products, with safety woven throughout.
- Behavioral questions have shifted — "Tell me about a failure" is now "Walk me through an AI product decision you made that seemed right but you'd approach differently now."
- The live build round is a **product judgment test that happens to involve a coding environment** — interviewers want to see judgment before any building starts; candidates who open their laptop immediately raise concern.
- Traditional product sense prep falls short because AI systems fail **probabilistically** (hallucinations, gradual drift) rather than with hard errors — metrics lag behind actual user trust erosion.
- At Anthropic, candidates who reach **minute 40 without addressing safety** have already failed in the interviewer's view — safety must be framed in from the start, not added at the end.
- Know the layer boundary: **model-layer problems** (accuracy, drift, retraining) belong to researchers; **application-layer problems** (communicating uncertainty, trust-building via design) are the PM's domain — interviewers score this distinction directly.
- Daily practice drill: ask a model to do something it's likely to get wrong (niche factual claim, edge-case calculation), then analyze where it broke, how it broke, and what that would mean inside a real product.
- Build-round protocol: **before asking the AI to write any code, ask it to describe its plan first** — review the plan, then proceed. Narrate decisions throughout ("I'm scoping to this because…", "I'm skipping X because under time pressure Y matters more.").
- Practice with a human who will push back — AI tools are too agreeable to be useful calibration partners for interview prep.
