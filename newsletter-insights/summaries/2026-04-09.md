## Aakash Gupta (Product Growth)

### The AI PM interview has changed. Here's what to expect. `AI/ML`
- Interview prep from 2023 no longer works in 2026—candidates studying the old test are getting rejected despite having cruised through PM loops in 2023
- Shift 1: Interviewers test whether you've *built* AI, not whether you understand it—they probe for architecture names, F1 scores, and business impact numbers to verify you drove the work
- Shift 2: Vibe coding is now a real round at Google India, Figma, Perplexity, v0, Netflix, Stripe—45-minute prototype challenges in Cursor, Bolt, or Lovable without reps will fail this
- Shift 3: AI product sense replaced traditional product sense—Google removed standalone technical interview for PMs, OpenAI made it a required round with focus on model-layer vs application-layer separation
- Shift 4: Behavioral questions got harder—"Tell me about a failed trade-off" became "Walk me through an AI decision you'd approach differently now" requiring AI-specific technical reasoning
- Shift 5: Safety is tested everywhere—Anthropic has dedicated safety round, OpenAI embeds it throughout, Google tests it in Googleyness; by minute 40 without mentioning safety, you've signaled you don't understand AI PM
- Coach has placed 30+ candidates at OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta, Amazon in last 12 months; built 3 practice tools (Claude Skill, Custom GPT, Gemini Gem) that grade responses

### Must-Know Cross-Cutting Concerns in API Development `Engineering`
- Cross-cutting concerns (authentication, logging, rate limiting, input validation) are invisible when working correctly but catastrophic when missing
- These concerns don't belong to any single endpoint and don't appear in usual product requirements—they're the invisible layer separating a collection of endpoints from production-ready systems
- The hardest part is ensuring uniform application across every single route an API exposes—this is what separates hobby projects from systems that survive production
- Cross-cutting concerns create architectural requirements that must be designed in, not bolted on later

## Product School

### TikTok VP of Product on Killing the E-Commerce Funnel and Closing Commerce Inside the Feed `Product`
- Every extra click costs half your traffic—traditional funnel loses 50% at each step (ad impression → click → landing → product page → add to cart → account creation → verification → payment)
- On-platform commerce is the structural fix: user discovers product in feed, clicks, is already logged in as user, experiences serendipitous discovery and purchase simultaneously (different architecture, not UX improvement)
- AI at catalog scale enables commerce viability: generative AI adds motion to product images, generates scripts from prompts, automates creator affiliate commissions through GMV Max
- Live shopping is a scheduled business operation, not casual broadcast: merchants announce in advance, run ads against scheduled broadcast, partner with creators who have embedded audiences, shift ad spend mid-stream toward performing products
- "Stores were doing a million dollars an hour off their live stream sales"—the merchants generating highest volume from live streams have nimble internal approval workflows
- "Ads ranking has been using AI forever—we called it machine learning, terminology evolved"—the technology isn't new, only the interface is
- Core insight: if you build monetization solution that honors user experience, revenue takes care of itself; question is whether you have infrastructure to make it true at scale
