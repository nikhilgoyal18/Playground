## Lenny Rachitsky (@lennysan) `Product`
*1 substantive tweet*

### Rabois: Hire Barrels, Not Ammunition — The Most Underused Hiring Framework `Business`
- Keith Rabois' framing: "most companies hire ammunition when they need barrels" — ammunition is people who execute tasks when directed; barrels are people who can independently own an entire initiative from concept to completion
- The failure mode: companies raise money, hire lots of people, burn rate increases, and the CEO gets frustrated — because they added execution capacity (ammo) without adding initiative ownership capacity (barrels)
- Barrel-to-ammunition ratio is a more useful diagnostic for team health than headcount or org chart structure; a company with 2 barrels and 50 ammo will outperform one with 0 barrels and 200 ammo
- Implication: As AI handles more execution tasks (ammo work), the scarcity and value of barrels increases; founders should ruthlessly hire for barrel traits (ownership, initiative, judgment) and let AI handle the rest
